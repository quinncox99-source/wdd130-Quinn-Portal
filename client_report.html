Me as a Client

As a client I made everything myself, I wrote up the text, I made the images in google drawings, and I came up with the wireframes.

I communicated through discord, my favourite kind of group chat, and I had a few issues communicating although that was mostly on my end as it took me a while to make all of the images.

I think the developers met all my expectations with this site, it is amazing and perfectly follows what I gave them.

Though I think next time I hire a team to do a project for me I will make sure I have all of the images and wireframes up front.

My learning experience as a Junior Dev

I forgot how to contact both of my project leaders, I forgot their faces, and did not ask for help when I could.

I was supposed to do one of the pages however I did not have access to communications and realized to late that this was something I need to fix.

I was not able to contribute to either of their sites and that is a fatal mistake that I will try to learn from.

I didnt want to bother the lead devs by making changes this late into the project and I would probably have to remake most of what other people did as I wouldnt understand their code well.

Overall

The most challenging part of this project was understanding other peoples code. Sadly I was not able to overcome this and either reinvented systems or went mad.

The thing I enjoyed most about this project is the exchange, where you get a project and make it with precision, where you have everything you need to make it and you just do it.

One of the main things I would do if I were to do this project again would be to not lose everyones contacts and get better at documentation and neat code.

Overall I would say this entire project has made me more confident at working on real-world projects becuase I now know what not to do, and some of the things that work.
